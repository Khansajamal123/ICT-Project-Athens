About Us

Athens is the capital and largest city of Greece. According to Greek mythology the city was named after Athena, the ancient Greek goddess of wisdom, but modern scholars generally agree that the goddess took her name after the city.

History

Athens has been continuously inhabited for at least 5,000 years (3000 BC). By 1400 BC, the settlement had become an important centre of the Mycenaean civilization, and the Acropolis was the site of a major Mycenaean fortress, whose remains can be recognised from sections of the characteristic Cyclopean walls.

Culture

The culture of Athens is a harmonious blend of ancient traditions and modern creativity, where art, philosophy, and history echo through every street.

Attractions

Athens boasts iconic attractions like the majestic Acropolis and the Parthenon, standing as timeless symbols of ancient glory. From the historic Plaka district to the vibrant Monastiraki Square, Athens offers a mesmerizing mix of history, culture, and modern charm.

Modern City

Athens is a dynamic modern city, seamlessly blending cutting-edge innovation with its rich historical legacy. Athens today is a cosmopolitan hub, where ancient heritage meets modern architecture and forward-thinking culture. With a history spanning over three millennia, Athens has been the heart of ancient civilizations, serving as a pivotal center for the Greek world and the birthplace of democracy. Its enduring legacy has made it a beacon of culture, philosophy, and influence throughout history.
